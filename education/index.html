What is ACM Education?

ACM Education was created to provide the additional academic and professional support needed for students to succeed outside of the classroom environment. The main goal for ACM Education is to offer programs andworkshops that allow students to build their technical skills and network with others to better prepare themselves for life after college.

Mentor Program

A year-long program meant to help kickstart the journey of ECS underclassmen.

What is the ACM Mentor Program?

The ACM Mentor Program is a year-long program that connects Freshmen and Sophomores to upperclassmen who are interested in similar fields as them. The program is grounded in the belief that we can create meaningful connections for the next generation of technologists to help them grow and succeed. Applications to join the program open near the beginning of Fall Semester every year.

Join the ACM Mentor Program!

Are you an overwhelmed ECS student who wants to meet people who actually have been there, done that? Want to learn how they snagged that cool internship, how they were able to ace their CS classes, or just how they survived college in general? Being an ACM mentee means having access to people who understand what you're going through, so let us help you get where you want to be.

Become a Mentor!

Do you wish you had known some things coming into UTD? Now you have a chance to guide freshmen and sophomores with your knowledge. You can help them recognize and reach their academic, personal, and career goals. Share your insight, give back to others, and sharpen your leadership skills by becoming a Mentor for the ACM Mentor Program! Applications to become a mentor open at the start of the Fall Semester.

Interview Prep Workshops

A series of workshops we hold throughout the semester in hopes of preparing attendees for technical interviews and coding challenges.

As CS/SE careers continue to expand and reach every corner of the globe, employers for top companies have become more and more stringent in their requirements for new talent. Almost all large employers have come to a similar conclusion that the critical thinking capacity of new hires and interns is best gauged through a series of technical interviews or coding challenges that present logical problems to students to solve through programming.

Many times, normal coursework is simply not enough to ace these types of interviews. To compensate for this deficiency, we created this workshop series to make UTD students more competitive in their job search.

Each week we cover a different data structure or technique that is frequently asked about during interviews. We first do a brief overview of the data structure/technique and then work through several Leetcode-style questions together involving it to gain a better understand its implementation. We leave each workshop with a collection of additional problems that attendees can do outside the workshop to solidify their understanding of the concepts discussed.

This workshop series is a perfect way for students to prepare for upcoming interviews and/or the general recruitment cycle.

The Interview Prep Workshop Series is held in collaboration with UTD Blockchain
